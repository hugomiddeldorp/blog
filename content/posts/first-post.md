My name is Hugo and I'm a pianist and teacher living in Auckland, New Zealand. While I love teaching and I have a lot of fun seeing young (and not so young) musicians grow and improve, I feel like I need a carreer change.

I've always been interested in programming and computer science, and I went to university for music on a whim.

I don't regret that decision.

That being said, it's time for me to radically change direction and go back to programming. I have (I think) a good base in Python, HTML and CSS, but there are so many gaps that I need to fill. My goal is to go through the equivalent of an undergrad computer science degree by myself.

As of right now, there are a few resources that I've consulted and that I'm starting to work through.

* [OSSU - Computer science](https://github.com/ossu/computer-science)
* [Teach Yourself CS](https://teachyourselfcs.com/)

My plans for the coming weeks is to brush up on Discrete Maths, learn C and start going through either [Computer Systems: A Programmer's Perspective](http://csapp.cs.cmu.edu/3e/home.html) or [The Elements of Computing Systems](https://www.nand2tetris.org/).
